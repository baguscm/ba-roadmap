# Talent Management

**Talent Management** represents the strategic side of HR, encompassing how an organization assesses, develops, and eventually parts ways with its workforce. This node focuses on aligning employee goals with corporate objectives, identifying high-potential individuals, and ensuring smooth transitions during offboarding.

## Node Knowledge: Development & Transitions
1. **Performance Management:** Setting cascading goals, conducting mid-year/annual appraisals, and executing 360-degree feedback.
2. **Compensation & Merit:** Utilizing performance data to drive annual salary increases, bonuses, and equity grants.
3. **Learning & Development (L&D):** Curating training programs, tracking certifications, and ensuring compliance training completion.
4. **Offboarding:** Processing voluntary or involuntary terminations, retrieving assets, and conducting exit interviews.

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

Aligned with *BABOK®*, this area leverages **Requirements Elicitation** and **Stakeholder Engagement** to handle highly subjective business processes.

*   **Workflow Design:** Charting the complex approval matrix for the annual focal point review process (e.g., Employee -> Manager -> Skip-Level -> HRBP).
*   **Calibration Session Mapping:** Defining how managers come together to view a "9-box grid" and normalize performance ratings across a department.
*   **Data Archiving Analysis:** Specifically for offboarding, determining what employee data must be retained for legal reasons vs. what access must be immediately revoked.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Performance Cycle Timeline:** A structured roadmap showing the rollout phases of goal setting vs. evaluations.
*   **Merit Matrix Rules:** The business logic connecting a performance rating to a suggested merit increase percentage scale.
*   **Offboarding Checklist / Workflow:** A sequenced diagram showing IT decommissioning, benefits termination, and final paycheck generation.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> During a Talent Management implementation, HR leaders often want to completely redesign their philosophy (e.g., ditching annual reviews for continuous feedback). 
> As a BA, be prepared to act as a change management consultant. Remind them that software configuration is fast, but changing corporate culture takes years. Ensure the system design isn't too rigid to accomadate future shifts in strategy.
