# Credit Management & Risk

In **Advanced B2B O2C**, **Credit Management** is a proactive financial filter. It ensures that the company limits its exposure to bad debt by assessing customer creditworthiness before orders are released to the warehouse.

## Node Knowledge: The Financial Guardrail
1. **Credit Limit:** The maximum value of open orders and invoices a customer can have.
2. **Risk Profiling:** Grouping customers by credit risk (Low, Mid, High) based on payment history and external ratings (e.g., D&B).
3. **Credit Block:** Automatically stopping an order if it exceeds the limit or if the customer has significantly overdue invoices.
4. **Credit Release Workflow:** The process where a Credit Manager reviews and manually approves a blocked order.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Risk Analysis:** Defining the mathematical logic that determines a customer's credit limit.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** Configuring the "Automatic Credit Check" triggers—determining *when* the system should check credit (e.g., at order entry vs. at delivery).

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Credit Review Workflow:** A flowchart showing the path of a blocked order.
*   **External Data Integration Spec:** Detailing how the ERP pulls credit scores from external agencies.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't make your credit rules too rigid. If you block an order for a loyal customer because they are $5 over their limit, you risk losing the relationship. Your BA logic should include "Credit Tolerances" and "Seasonal Overrides" to allow for business flexibility while still maintaining financial control.
