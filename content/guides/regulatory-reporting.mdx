# Regulatory Reporting

Specifically within the "External Financial Reporting" APQC category for financial institutions.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Data Aggregation:** Consolidating global transaction and customer risk data into a reporting table.
2. **Field Mapping:** Reconciling internal platform codes with "Official" government filing headers.
3. **Pre-Filing Validation:** Logic-based checks to ensure no null values or bad date formats before submission.
4. **Electronic Submission:** Secure, encrypted transmission to the regulator (e.g. GoAML or FinCEN portals).

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Document Analysis:** Deconstructing hundreds of pages of government filing specifications (e.g., NACHA, ISO 20022).
*   **Requirement Management:** Maintaining different form requirements for local, state, and global jurisdictions.
*   **Data Lineage Mapping:** Proving the path of a reported number from the ledger to the final filing.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Regulatory Data Field Mapping Matrix**
*   **Annual Filing Calendar / SOP**
*   **Data Audit Trail Documentation**

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Data Lineage is your shield. If a regulator audits your SAR filings, as a BA you must be able to prove exactly how a reported figure was calculated. Always document the RAW SQL used for reports.
