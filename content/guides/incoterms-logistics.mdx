# Incoterms & Freight Liability

In **Advanced P2P with Import**, **Incoterms** (International Commercial Terms) are the legal "DNA" of a purchase. They define the exact moment when the risk and the cost of the goods transfer from the seller to the buyer during international transit.

## Node Knowledge: The Risk Boundary
1. **Incoterms Definition:** Standard rules (e.g., EXW, FOB, CIF, DDP) published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC).
2. **Transfer of Risk:** Identifying the physical point (e.g., "Loaded on Vessel") where the buyer becomes responsible if the goods are lost or damaged.
3. **Transfer of Cost:** Identifying who pays for freight, insurance, and customs duties.
4. **Freight Booking:** The operational process of securing space on a ship or airplane via a Freight Forwarder.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "FOB" (Free On Board). Many buyers assume it means they own the goods once they leave the factory. It actually means ownership transfers once they are *on the ship*. If the factory burns down with your finished goods inside, and you used FOB, the loss is the vendor's, not yours. As a BA, ensure your "Inventory Valuation" logic matches your legal Incoterms.
