# Asset Depreciation Setup

In **Advanced P2P with Assets**, **Depreciation** is the systematic allocation of an asset's cost over its "Useful Life." It is a non-cash expense that is critical for accurate financial reporting and tax calculations.

## Node Knowledge: The Value Erosion
1. **Useful Life:** The expected duration (e.g., 5 years for IT, 20 years for buildings) the asset will provide value.
2. **Depreciation Methods:** Straight-line (equal amounts), Declining Balance (accelerated), or Units-of-Production.
3. **Salvage Value:** The estimated residual value of the asset at the end of its useful life.
4. **Depreciation Keys:** The configuration in the ERP that automates the monthly posting to the General Ledger.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Retirement Bias". Many companies forget to "Scrap" assets in the system when they are physically thrown away. This leads to an overinflated balance sheet and paying insurance on ghosts. As a BA, you should implement a "Physical Audit" requirement at least once a year to sync the ERP with reality.
