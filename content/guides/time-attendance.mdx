# Time & Attendance

The **Time & Attendance** process captures, validates, and manages employee working hours and absences. This node is a critical precursor to payroll, ensuring that hourly personnel are compensated accurately and that salaried employees' leave balances are tracked in accordance with enterprise policies and labor laws.

## Node Knowledge: Time Tracking Dynamics
1. **Time Capture:** Recording punches via physical clocks, web portals, or mobile apps.
2. **Scheduling & Shifts:** Defining typical working hours, shift differentials, and predictive scheduling.
3. **Absence Management:** Requesting, approving, and accruing PTO, sick leave, and statutory leaves (e.g., FMLA).
4. **Time Evaluation:** Applying business rules to raw punches to determine regular hours, overtime, and penalty pay.

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

This area heavily utilizes **Requirements Elicitation** and **Business Rules Analysis** from the *BABOK®* guide.

*   **Overtime Rule Definition:** Translating complex union agreements or state laws (e.g., California daily overtime) into system calculation rules.
*   **Process Mapping:** Charting the approval workflow for timesheets and PTO requests, including delegations when managers are absent.
*   **Integration Analysis:** Ensuring that the approved, evaluated time block data flows seamlessly to the payroll gross-to-net engine.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Calculated Time Rules Document:** Complex matrices detailing how time punches are mapped to specific pay codes (Regular, OT 1.5, Double Time, Shift Premium).
*   **Leave Accrual Matrix:** Defining how much PTO is earned per pay period based on an employee's tenure and job level.
*   **Exceptions Workflow:** Diagrams mapping how missing punches or late arrivals are flagged and resolved by supervisors.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!CAUTION]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Time evaluation is where lawsuits are born. State specific meal-break compliance is exceedingly difficult to configure correctly. 
> Ensure your requirements capture not just the "rule," but how the system handles the breach of a rule. If a California employee takes a 25-minute lunch instead of 30, how is the penalty configured, and who is notified?
