# Credit Scoring

A central pillar of "Evaluate and Manage Credit Risk" (APQC 7.2) for lenders.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Bureau Pull:** Retrieving historical credit files from Experian, Equifax, or TransUnion via API.
2. **Feature Engineering:** Calculating derived ratios like "Utilization Rate" and "Account Longevity."
3. **Model Execution:** Running the data through the proprietary or 3rd-party risk algorithm.
4. **Adverse Action:** Informing the customer and regulator of the decision and the "Reason Codes" for the score.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Business Rules Analysis:** Documenting the precise "weighting" of different credit factors.
*   **Data Profiling:** Ensuring the incoming credit data feeds are consistent and not "noisy."
*   **Acceptance Criteria:** Defining the "Cut-off" scores for "Tier 1," "Tier 2," etc.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Financial Inclusion is a business driver. As a BA, recommend "Alternative Data" (e.g., utility bills, rent payments) for scoring customers who are "Credit Invisible" to the Big Three bureaus.

## Enterprise Software Implementation Reference

*   **Experian (Boost):** A modern data enhancement service designed to update consumer credit scores using positive payment history.
