# Letter of Credit & Trade Finance

In **Advanced P2P with Import**, the **Letter of Credit (L/C)** is a critical financial anchor. It acts as a guarantee from the buyer's bank to the seller's bank that payment will be made provided that certain conditions (shipment proof, document quality) are met.

## Node Knowledge: The Financial Guarantee
1. **L/C Issuance:** The buyer's bank carves out credit to guarantee the purchase.
2. **Documentary Collection:** The seller must provide "Clean" documents (Bill of Lading, Invoice, Packing List) to the bank to trigger payment.
3. **Discrepancy Handling:** Managing cases where the documents provided do not perfectly match the L/C terms.
4. **Negotiation & Settlement:** The final transfer of funds once compliance is verified by both banking institutions.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Financial Analysis:** Analyzing the cost of financing vs. the risk of cross-border payment default.
*   **Requirements Modeling:** ensuring that the ERP can handle "Payment on Document Receipt" logic, which differs from standard net-30 terms.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **L/C Request Form:** The internal document used to initiate a credit guarantee with the Treasury department.
*   **Document Checklist:** A rigid list of shipping data points that must be verified before payment is authorized.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Letters of Credit are pedantic. A single typo in a "Bill of Lading" can cause a bank to reject a $1M payment. As a BA, you must automate the "Document Drafting" process using ERP data to ensure that what we *asked* for in the L/C matches exactly what the vendor *puts* on their shipping documents.
