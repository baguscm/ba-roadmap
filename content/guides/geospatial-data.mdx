# Geospatial Data Mgt

A critical knowledge node, mapped to "Manage Knowledge and Information" (APQC 12.0) and "Execute Exploration" (APQC 4.0) in the mining context.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Data Acquisition:** capturing spatial information through satellite imagery, drone flyovers, and physical GPS surveying.
2. **GIS Layering:** aggregating disparate data types (Topography, Geophysics, Hydrology, Tenure) into a unified Geographic Information System (GIS).
3. **Spatial Analysis:** using algorithms to identify mineralization patterns and calculate volumetric estimates.
4. **Tenure Management:** tracking the physical boundaries of mining leases and ensuring geospatial compliance with government licenses.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Interface Analysis:** designing the "Single Pane of Glass" dashboard that combines real-time GPS asset locations with static geological maps.
*   **Data Modelling:** mapping the complex coordinate systems (WGS84, UTM) and ensuring compatibility across different geospatial software.
*   **Solution Evaluation:** verifying that the accuracy of drone-captured data meets the standards required for block-model updates.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't forget the "Z" axis. As a BA, ensure your geospatial models are truly 3D. In Mining, the depth (Z) is just as important as the X and Y coordinates. If your data model doesn't support sub-surface layering, you can't accurately plan the extraction.
