# Procure to Pay (P2P)

According to **APQC** standards, the Procure to Pay (P2P) process falls under the "Procure Materials and Services" framework. It encompasses the end-to-end cycle of identifying a need for goods, sourcing them from suppliers, receiving them into inventory, and issuing payment. In e-commerce, executing P2P efficiently prevents stockouts and manages working capital.

## Node Knowledge: The P2P Lifecycle
1. **Sourcing & Requisition:** Determining what inventory is needed based on demand forecasting.
2. **Purchasing (PO Creation):** Formalizing the order with the supplier.
3. **Receiving:** Taking physical delivery of goods and capturing data.
4. **Invoicing & Payment:** Matching supplier invoices with internal POs and receipts to authorize payment.

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mapping the P2P lifecycle heavily relies on the **Process Analysis** and **Elicitation and Collaboration** knowledge areas.

*   **Process Modeling:** Documenting the "As-Is" flow of how a buyer requests inventory vs the "To-Be" automated process.
*   **Gap Analysis:** Identifying where manual interventions (like paper invoices) are slowing down the procure-to-pay cycle.
*   **Stakeholder Identification:** Mapping the complex web of stakeholders (Buyers, Warehouse Managers, Accounts Payable clerks).

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Process Flow Diagrams (BPMN):** Defining swimlanes for Procurement, Warehouse, and Finance.
*   **Business Rules Document:** Capturing threshold rules (e.g., "Purchases over $50k require VP approval").

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Your biggest challenge in P2P implementation isn't the requirements—it's the data silos. 
> * Procurement lives in negotiating contracts. 
> * Warehouse lives in physical boxes. 
> * Finance lives in the ledger. 
> As a BA, you must force these three groups into the same room. A broken P2P process usually means one department designed the workflow without consulting the others.

## Enterprise Software Implementation Reference

*   **SAP S/4HANA & Ariba:** Typically, sourcing occurs in Ariba (Upstream), while the actual physical receiving and invoice matching occurs in S/4HANA (Downstream).
*   **Oracle NetSuite:** NetSuite handles P2P natively via the "Procure to Pay" suite, allowing a single continuous workflow from the `Purchase Order` record to the `Item Receipt` sub-record, mapping to the `Vendor Bill`.
