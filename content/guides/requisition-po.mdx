# Requisition to PO

The **Procure to Pay (P2P)** process begins with the identification of a need. **Requisition to PO** covers the journey from an internal employee's request to the issuance of a legally binding Purchase Order (PO) to a vendor.

## Node Knowledge: The Commitment Trigger
1. **Purchase Requisition (PR):** An internal document used by an employee to request goods or services.
2. **Approval Workflow:** Routing the PR through budget holders and management based on cost centers.
3. **Sourcing/Catalog Selection:** Choosing the correct item from a pre-negotiated contract or catalog.
4. **Purchase Order (PO):** The financial and legal commitment sent to the vendor.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Financial Analysis:** Ensuring that the requested purchase is within the assigned budget for the department.
*   **Requirements Validation:** Ensuring the "Description of Goods" on the PO is specific enough to be used later for Quality Inspection and Invoicing.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Approval Matrix:** A logic table showing who needs to sign off on a purchase based on its value.
*   **Functional Specification:** For the integration between the Procurement system and the Finance Ledger.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Minimize "Maverick Spending"—the practice of employees buying outside of approved catalogs. As a BA, your goal is to make the internal requisition process as easy as an e-commerce shopping experience. If it's too hard to use, employees will buy via credit card, causing the company to lose negotiating power and budget control.
