# Customer Billing & CIS

Maps to "Manage Customer Service" (APQC 3.0) and "Manage Financial Resources" (APQC 9.0) in the Utilities sector.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Meter-to-Cash:** Capturing the raw usage data from the "Advanced Metering Infrastructure" (AMI) and translating it into a bill.
2. **Tariff Configuration:** managing the complex "Rate Schedules" which vary based on time-of-use (TOU), tiering, and season.
3. **Account Receivables (AR):** managing the collection of funds, payment plan arrangements, and statutory disconnection processes.
4. **CRM & Portal Integration:** giving customers real-time visibility into their usage and the ability to manage their accounts via self-service.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Business Rules Analysis:** mapping the legal requirement for "Cold Weather Policies" where utilities cannot be disconnected during winter months.
*   **Data Modelling:** designing the data schema to support "Net Metering" (where customers sell solar power back to the grid).
*   **Stakeholder Collaboration:** reconciling the Legal team's "Compliance" requirements with Finance's "Cash Flow" goals.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Focus on "Bill Transparency." As a BA, when the utility changes its rates, ensures the system can explain the change to the customer on their bill. A "Confused" customer is a customer who doesn't pay and calls the helpdesk, increasing your costs.
