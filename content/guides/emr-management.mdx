# EMR/EHR Records Mgt

The core clinical node, mapped to "Execute Patient Logistics & Care" (APQC 7.2) and "Manage Health Information" (APQC 12.0).

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Clinical Documentation:** Capturing structured and unstructured provider notes (SOAP notes) during patient encounters.
2. **Order Entry (CPOE):** managing "Computerized Physician Order Entry" for labs, imaging, and prescriptions.
3. **Medication Reconciliation:** Ensuring the patient's medication list is accurate across every transition of care.
4. **Data Interoperability:** Exchanging clinical data between different hospital systems using HL7 FHIR standards.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Observation:** conducting "Shadowing" sessions with clinicians to understand the "Click-burden" and UX bottlenecks.
*   **Non-Functional Requirements:** defining the critical "High-Availability" and millisecond response-time requirements for emergency care systems.
*   **Concept Modelling:** mapping the relationships between Patients, Providers, Encounters, and Clinical Observations.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Alert Fatigue." As a BA, don't design a system that pops up a warning for everything. If doctors get 100 alerts a day, they will start ignoring the 1 alert that actually matters for patient safety. Measure and optimize the "Actionability" of every clinical alert.
