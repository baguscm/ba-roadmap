# Inventory Optimization

Falls under "Manage Inventory" (APQC 6.3). This node focuses on the mathematical balance between working capital and service levels.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Demand Sensing:** Capturing real-time sales signals to adjust replenishment cycles.
2. **Safety Stock Calculation:** Using standard deviation and service level targets to define the "Buffer."
3. **ABC/XYZ Segmentation:** Categorizing items by value (ABC) and predictability (XYZ) to prioritize management effort.
4. **VMI Synchronization:** Managing "Vendor Managed Inventory" where suppliers hold responsibility for replenishment triggers.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Risk Analysis:** modeling the impact of a "Stock-out" on customer retention vs. the cost of holding excess inventory.
*   **Data Analysis:** analyzing historical lead times to identify supplier variability.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** defining the "Auto-replenishment" thresholds to remove manual purchasing friction.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't optimize in a silo. As a BA, ensure your inventory targets are linked to the Sales & Operations Planning (S&OP) cycle. If Marketing plans a 50% discount campaign and Supply Chain doesn't increase reach, you'll have a 100% stock-out rate.
