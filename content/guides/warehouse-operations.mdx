# Warehouse Operations

Relates to "Manage Logistics and Warehousing" (APQC 6.4), focusing on internal storage efficiency and flow.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Inventory Slotting:** Placing popular items (fast-movers) closer to packing stations to reduce travel time.
2. **Replenishment:** Moving stock from "Overstock" bulk storage to "Active Picking" bins.
3. **Cycle Counting:** Regular, scheduled audits of stock to maintain inventory accuracy without total shutdowns.
4. **Transfer Orders:** Moving stock between warehouses in a multi-node fulfillment network.

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Organization Modelling:** Defining warehouse supervisor vs. warehouse picker roles and permissions.
*   **Decision Analysis:** Choosing between manual picking vs. automated SOR (Sortation) systems.
*   **Interface Analysis:** Mapping WMS data (Bin, Lot, Serial) into the ERP for financial ledger updates.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Bin/Zone Mapping Document**
*   **Replenishment Trigger Rules Matrix**
*   **Warehouse Picking Productivity Dashboard Spec**

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Slotting is dynamic. As a BA, ensure your system uses sales velocity data to suggest bin moves. What was a top seller in July (swimwear) shouldn't be in the prime picking zone in December.
