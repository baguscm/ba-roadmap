# Fixed Asset Capitalization

In **Advanced P2P with Assets**, **Capitalization** is the process of converting a purchase (Expense) into a long-term balance sheet item (Fixed Asset). This happens when a purchase meets the company's "Capitalization Threshold" for value and useful life.

## Node Knowledge: The Balance Sheet Conversion
1. **Capitalization Hierarchy:** Defining the structure (Main Asset vs. Sub-Assets).
2. **Value Inclusion:** Determining which costs (Shipping, Installation, Training) can be added to the asset's "Base Value."
3. **Asset Master Data:** Capturing serial numbers, physical location, and responsible department.
4. **Capital Work in Progress (CWIP):** Managing assets that are being "built" over time before being finalized.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Bundle Purchases". If you buy 100 laptops for $1,000 each, but your threshold is $5,000, do you capitalize the bundle? Usually, no—capitalization is per physical unit. As a BA, you must define the "Unit of Measure" for your asset policies to prevent auditors from flagging thousands of small expenses that are currently sitting on your balance sheet.
