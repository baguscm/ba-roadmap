# Analytics

According to **APQC**, Analytics fits under "Develop Enterprise Performance Management (EPM)". Data is the lifeblood of modern e-commerce. Analytics is the rigorous process of extracting raw data generated by the Storefront, OMS, WMS, and ERP, transforming it, and modeling it into actionable business intelligence that drives strategic decision-making.

## Node Knowledge: The Analytics Lifecycle
1. **Data Ingestion (ETL/ELT):** Extracting raw data from disparate systems via APIs and loading it into a central repository.
2. **Data Warehousing:** Storing data efficiently in a centralized, structured database architecture.
3. **Data Modeling/Transformation:** Cleaning the data, resolving format discrepancies, and building relationships (e.g., tying a Google Analytics Session ID to a Shopify Order ID).
4. **Data Visualization:** Building dashboards that executives and operators can consume to monitor KPIs.

---

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, this area heavily leverages **Data Mining**, **Metrics and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**, and **Data Dictionary** techniques.

*   **Requirements Elicitation:** Interviewing executives to determine *what* questions they need answered, before touching any data. (e.g., "I need to know our Customer Acquisition Cost by region").
*   **Data Lineage Mapping:** Documenting exactly where a specific data point originates. If the dashboard shows 500 sales, the BA must document the exact database query path proving where that "500" came from.
*   **Non-Functional Requirements:** Establishing data latency rules. Does the dashboard need to be "Real-Time" (expensive), or is a "T-1" (yesterday's data refreshed nightly) acceptable?

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Source-to-Target Mapping (STTM):** A massive spreadsheet mapping raw source database fields to their final destination fields in the data warehouse.
*   **Mockups:** Visual wireframes of the dashboard layout designed before the data engineers build the backend pipeline.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> **Clarify the "Source of Truth".**
> In e-commerce, Google Analytics might say you made $100,000 yesterday, while Shopify says $95,000, and your ERP says $98,000. *They are all correct based on their own definitions.* (e.g., GA tracks clicks, Shopify tracks carts, ERP tracks shipped boxes). 
> The BA's most important job is getting the business to formally agree on which system is the ultimate "Source of Truth" for executive reporting.
