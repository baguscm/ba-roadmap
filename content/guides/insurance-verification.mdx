# Insurance Verification

Directly supports "Manage Financial Resources" (APQC 9.0) and "Verify Insurance Eligibility" (APQC 7.2.2). This is the primary driver of Revenue Cycle integrity.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Eligibility Check:** Using EDI 270/271 transactions to verify active coverage in real-time with the payer.
2. **Prior Authorization:** Submitting clinical documentation to the insurance company to obtain approval for specialized procedures.
3. **Benefits Coordination:** Determining which insurance is "Primary" vs "Secondary" in multi-payer scenarios.
4. **Estimation:** Calculating the patient's "Out-of-pocket" responsibility (Deductible, Co-pay) before the service is rendered.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Interface Analysis:** mapping the EDI 270 (Eligibility Inquiry) and 271 (Response) fields to the internal hospital registrar screen.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** defining the logic for "Medical Necessity" as per individual payer contracts.
*   **Decision Analysis:** using decision trees to automatically flag high-cost procedures that require immediate Prior-Authorization.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Focus on "Clean Claims." As a BA, your goal is to prevent a claim from ever being mailed if the insurance isn't verified. 90% of healthcare billing denials are preventable at the "front-end" insurance verification stage. Build hard-stops into the system for missing authorization.
