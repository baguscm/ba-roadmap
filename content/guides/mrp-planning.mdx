# MRP & Materials Planning

**Materials Requirement Planning (MRP)** is the engine of manufacturing. It calculates exactly what ingredients (raw materials) and components are needed, in what quantity, and by when, to meet the finished goods production schedule.

## Node Knowledge: The Supply Calculation
1. **Master Production Schedule (MPS):** The "statement of intent"—what finished goods we plan to build.
2. **Bill of Materials (BOM):** The "recipe"—a structured list of every component needed for one unit.
3. **Inventory Status:** Real-time visibility into on-hand, on-order, and reserved stock.
4. **Lead Times:** Calculating the time required to procure or manufacture each component.
5. **MRP Run:** The automated calculation that generates "Planned Orders" (for production) and "Purchase Requisitions" (for procurement).

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Requirements Analysis:** Defining the "Lot Sizing" rules—for example, do we buy exactly what we need, or in bulk quantities to get discounts?
*   **Solution Evaluation:** Analyzing the accuracy of the inventory data—if the system thinks we have stock that isn't really there, MRP will fail.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **BOM Structure Map:** A visual hierarchy of finished goods down to raw materials.
*   **MRP Exception Guide:** A desk procedure for planners to handle system flags (e.g., "Cancel Order", "Reschedule-In").

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Garbage in, garbage out. The success of MRP depends 100% on two numbers: **BOM Accuracy** and **Inventory Accuracy**. If your BOM says you need 5 screws but the product needs 6, or if the system says you have 100 screws but you only have 80, your production line will stop. As a BA, focus your efforts on the "Cycle Counting" and "BOM Governance" processes first.
