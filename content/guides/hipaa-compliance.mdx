# HIPAA & Privacy Compliance

The regulatory bedrock of Healthcare BA work, mapped to "Manage Regulatory Compliance" (APQC 11.0) and "Maintain Information Security" (APQC 12.0).

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA):** Systematically evaluating new software or processes for risks to "Protected Health Information" (PHI).
2. **Access Control (RBAC):** Implementing "Role-Based Access Control" to ensure only authorized clinical staff can view specific patient records ("Minimum Necessary" rule).
3. **Audit Monitoring:** maintaining immutable logs of who viewed what record, when, and from where.
4. **Partner Management (BAA):** ensuring every vendor with access to PHI has a signed "Business Associate Agreement" (BAA) and a verified security posture.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Risk Analysis:** assigning "High," "Medium," or "Low" risk scores to different PHI data elements.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** defining the "Minimum Necessary" data set required for a specific business function (e.g. Billing vs Clinical Care).
*   **Document Analysis:** verifying that third-party SOC-2 reports and security questionnaires meet the enterprise privacy standard.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Privacy is not just "No." As a BA, don't just block data access. Solve the problem by designing "De-identified" data sets. If the Research team needs 10,000 records, give them the data without the Names and SSNs. You enable the business while protecting the patient.
