# Medical Billing & Claims

Mapped to "Manage Financial Resources" (APQC 9.0) and "Execute Billing & Claims" (APQC 7.2.2). This is the high-volume transaction layer of Healthcare.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Charge Capture:** Aggregating clinical services, medications, and equipment usage from the EMR for billing conversion.
2. **Coding (ICD-10/CPT):** Translating clinical descriptions into standardized medical codes to ensure payer processing.
3. **Claim Scrubbing:** Validating claims against thousands of "Payer-Specific Rules" to find errors before submission.
4. **Claims Submission (EDI 837):** Transmitting the digital 837P (Professional) or 837I (Institutional) file to the insurance clearinghouse.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Interface Analysis:** mapping the flow of "Charges" from the clinical EMR to the financial Billing system.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** documenting the "Modifier" rules (e.g. Modifier 25) that change the reimbursement value of a claim.
*   **Data Analysis:** tracking the "First Pass Yield" (claims paid on the first submission) to identify systemic coding errors.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Coding is the bottleneck. As a BA, your goal is "Auto-coding" where possible. If a clinician enters a specific "Procedure" in the EMR, the system should suggest the most likely CPT code automatically. Reducing manual coding reduces human error and speeds up your cash flow.
