# Accounts Receivable (A/R) & Collections

**Accounts Receivable (A/R)** represents the final stage of the **Order to Cash** lifecycle. It involves managing the funds owed by customers, reconciling payments, and proactively recovering overdue debt to maintain corporate liquidity.

## Node Knowledge: The Liquidity Management
1. **Payment Receipt:** Capturing funds via ACH, Check, Wire, or Credit Card.
2. **Cash Application:** Matching the payment to the open invoice in the ERP.
3. **Dunning:** The automated process of sending reminders for overdue invoices.
4. **Dispute Management:** Handling customer claims regarding short-payments or pricing errors.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Risk Analysis:** Using historical data to predict which customers are likely to default.
*   **Requirements Analysis:** Defining the logic for "Automatic Clearing"—how the system should behave when a payment doesn't perfectly match any invoice.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Dunning Flowchart:** Detailing the timing and severity of collection communications.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Focus on "Cash Application" efficiency. If your finance team is manually matching every dollar, they are wasting thousands of hours. Your goal as a BA is to achieve 90%+ "Auto-Match" by ensuring standard bank file formats (like BAI2 or CAMT.053) are correctly mapped to your ERP.
