# Customs & Regulatory Clearance

In **Advanced P2P with Import**, **Customs Clearance** is the critical boundary between international logistics and domestic inventory. It involves the legal declaration of goods to government authorities, ensuring that appropriate duties and taxes are paid and that all security and safety regulations are met.

## Node Knowledge: The Import Gateway
1. **Harmonized System (HS) Codes:** Classifying products correctly to determine duty rates and restricted goods.
2. **Import Declaration:** Submitting the formal "Entry" documentation to Customs (e.g., CBP in the USA).
3. **Duties & Fees:** Calculating and paying the tariffs based on the goods' value and origin.
4. **Inspections & Holds:** Managing physical inspections or document audits by regulatory agencies.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Requirements Validation:** Ensuring the ERP captures all data required for a legal customs entry (e.g., Country of Origin, Net Weight).
*   **Risk Analysis:** Assessing the financial impact of incorrect classification, which can lead to heavy fines and shipment seizures.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Import Compliance Manual:** Internal guidelines for how to classify and declare imported materials.
*   **Customs Data Map:** showing the flow of data from the Vendor Invoice to the Customs Entry form.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't rely on your freight forwarder to get the HS Codes right. They are logistics experts, but you are the product expert. As a BA, you should implement a "Master Classification" database within your ERP to ensure consistent, legally-defensible tax declarations every time you import a component.
