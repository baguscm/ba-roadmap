# Invoice Verification (The 3-Way Match)

**Invoice Verification** is the critical control point in the **Procure to Pay (P2P)** process. It ensures that the company only pays for what it ordered and what it actually received, preventing fraud, overbilling, and accounting errors.

## Node Knowledge: The Financial Control
1. **The 3-Way Match:** Automatically comparing the **Purchase Order** (what we asked for), the **Goods Receipt** (what we got), and the **Vendor Invoice** (what they billed).
2. **Tolerance Limits:** Determining how much of a price or quantity variance is allowed before the invoice is flagged for review.
3. **Price Variance:** Handling discrepancies where the billed price differs from the PO price.
4. **Quantity Variance:** Handling cases where the billed quantity exceeds the received quantity.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't manually reconcile "Small Dollar" variances. For example, if a vendor bills $0.50 more on a $5,000 order, the cost of a human reviewing that hold is much higher than the $0.50 loss. As a BA, you should implement "Automatic Write-offs" for variances under a certain threshold to keep the high-volume process moving.
