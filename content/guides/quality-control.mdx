# Quality Control & Inspection

In **Plan to Produce**, **Quality Control (QC)** is the regulatory and brand guardrail. It involves the systematic inspection of raw materials, in-process goods, and finished products to ensure they meet engineering specifications and safety standards before being released to customers.

## Node Knowledge: The Compliance Filter
1. **Inspection Plans:** Defining *what* to check (Dimensions, Weight, Color, Chemical composition) and the "Acceptable Quality Level" (AQL).
2. **In-Process Inspection:** Testing products during the manufacturing cycle (e.g., checking a car's alignment before the seats are installed).
3. **Quality Notifications:** Formalizing the process when an item fails a test—triggering a "Rework" or "Scrap" decision.
4. **Certificates of Analysis (CoA):** generating the legal documentation that proves a product meets the specs for the customer.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Risk Analysis:** Assessing the "Cost of Poor Quality"—identifying the financial impact of a product recall vs. the cost of more rigorous testing.
*   **Requirements Modeling:** Defining the "Calibration" requirements for the testing instruments used on the floor.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Inspection Flowchart:** showing the decision tree for "Pass" vs. "Fail" vs. "Rework".
*   **Quality SPC Dashboard:** Visualizing "Statistical Process Control" (e.g., tracking a machine's drift before it starts making bad parts).

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't inspect everything. "100% Inspection" is slow and expensive. As a BA, your goal is to help the business implement "Risk-Based Sampling". If a vendor has been perfect for 5 years, inspect only 1% of their goods. If a machine is starting to show variance, increase the testing frequency. Automation is your friend here—use sensors and computer vision where possible.
