# Invoicing & Billing

**Billing** is the transition point from supply chain activities to financial recognition. It is the process of generating a legal commercial invoice to the customer for the goods delivered or services rendered in the **O2C** cycle.

## Node Knowledge: The Revenue Trigger
1. **Billing Due List:** Monitoring orders and deliveries that are ready for invoicing.
2. **Invoice Generation:** Aggregating delivery data, pricing, and tax into a legal document.
3. **Accounting Integration:** Posting the invoice to the General Ledger (GL) as Revenue and Accounts Receivable (AR).
4. **Document Output:** Distributing the invoice via Email, Portal, or EDI/XML.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Financial Analysis:** Ensuring that the amounts calculated on the invoice match the Sales Order and the physical Shipment (The "3-Way Match" in reverse).
*   **External Design:** Designing the layouts of the invoices to meet various regional legal requirements (e.g., VAT in Europe vs. Sales Tax in US).

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Revenue Mapping Document:** Showing which GL accounts are hit when a specific product category is billed.
*   **Functional Specification:** For the automated billing batch jobs.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't assume all billing is "Ship-then-Bill". Modern businesses use "Subscription Billing", "Milestone Billing", and "Pro-forma Billing". As a BA, you must clarify the *trigger* for the invoice. If you bill too early, the customer rejects it; if you bill too late, you hurt the company's cash flow.
