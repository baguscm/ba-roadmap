# EDI & B2B Integration

In **Advanced B2B O2C**, **Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)** is the backbone of operational efficiency. It allows for the machine-to-machine exchange of business documents (Orders, Invoices, ASNs) between the company and its high-volume trading partners.

## Node Knowledge: The Automation Backbone
1. **Standard Protocols:** Using industry standards like EDIFACT, ANSI X12, or XML.
2. **Transaction Sets:** 850 (Purchase Order), 855 (PO Acknowledgment), 856 (Advance Ship Notice), 810 (Invoice).
3. **Data Mapping:** The process of translating the partner's data format into the company's ERP field structure.
4. **AS2 & VAN:** The communication methods used to securely transmit the data.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Interface Analysis:** Defining the specific data fields that must be mapped between the two systems.
*   **Requirements Validation:** Ensuring that the ERP has enough data to fulfill an order received via EDI without manual touchpoints.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Mapping Specification:** A field-by-field guide for developers to translate EDI segments into ERP tables.
*   **Error Exception Flowchart:** Detailing what happens when an EDI message fails validation.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't over-customize EDI for a single partner. If every customer has a different mapping, you create a maintenance nightmare. As a BA, your goal is to push for a "Global EDI Standard" internally and use middleware (like Boomi or MuleSoft) to handle the translation, keeping your core ERP clean.
