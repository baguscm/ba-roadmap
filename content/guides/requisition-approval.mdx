# Requisition & Approval

The **Recruit to Onboard** lifecycle begins with **Requisition & Approval**. This is the process of formalizing the need for a new hire, ensuring it aligns with the company's headcount budget, and securing the necessary executive sign-offs before the role is advertised.

## Node Knowledge: The Headcount Trigger
1. **Position Management:** Defining the job profile, grade, salary range, and reporting structure.
2. **Headcount Budgeting:** Verifying if the role is a "Replacement" (budgeted) or "New Growth" (incremental).
3. **Justification:** Documenting the business case for adding the resource.
4. **Multi-level Approval:** Routing the request through Department Heads, Finance, and HR Business Partners.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Financial Analysis:** Analyzing the total cost of hire (including benefits and overhead) against the available budget.
*   **Requirements Modeling:** ensuring that the mandatory data fields for a new requisition (e.g., Remote status, Essential skills) are standardized for the Applicant Tracking System (ATS).

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Approval Workflow Map:** showing the sequence of sign-offs required based on the role's seniority or salary.
*   **Requisition System Spec:** Detailing the integration between the Budgeting system and the Recruiting system.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Requisition bottlenecks usually happen in Finance. If your Finance team doesn't have real-time visibility into "Vacant Headcount", they will block recruiters from hiring, causing the business to lose talent. As a BA, your goal is to bridge the gap between HR and Finance by ensuring they share a single "Position Management" database.
