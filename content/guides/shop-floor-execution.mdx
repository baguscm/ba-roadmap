# Shop Floor Execution

**Shop Floor Execution** is where the plan becomes physical reality. It involves the coordination of labor, materials, and machines to convert manufacturing orders into finished goods while capturing real-time progress data for the ERP.

## Node Knowledge: The Production Pulse
1. **Production Orders:** The legal authority to manufacture a specific quantity on a specific date.
2. **Material Staging:** Bringing the raw materials from the warehouse to the production line.
3. **Goods Issue (GI):** Recording the consumption of raw materials against the order.
4. **Operations Confirmation:** Reporting the actual labor hours and machine time spent on each step.
5. **Goods Receipt (GR):** Putting the finished product into inventory once production is complete.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Process Analysis:** Identifying "Non-Value Added" activities on the shop floor (e.g., employees waiting for materials).
*   **Requirements Validation:** Ensuring the "Shop Floor User Interface" (MES) is simple enough for operators to use while wearing gloves or in a loud environment.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Execution Workflow:** showing the steps from Order Release -> Staging -> Consumption -> Confirmation -> Receipt.
*   **Scrap Mapping:** Detailing how lost material is accounted for financially.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Minimize "Paper on the Floor". If operators are writing their time down on slips of paper to be entered by a clerk later, your data is already 24 hours old. Your goal as a BA is to implement "Point-of-Action" data capture—using tablets, barcodes, or IoT sensors to get real-time feedback on production status.
