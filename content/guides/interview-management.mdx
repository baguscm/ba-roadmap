# Interview Management

**Interview Management** is the selection stage of the **Recruitment** lifecycle. It involves coordinating schedules between candidates and hiring teams, providing a standardized scoring framework to the interviewers, and managing the decision-making process to ensure fairness and quality of hire.

## Node Knowledge: The Selection Framework
1. **Interview Sequencing:** Screening call -> Technical/Competency interview -> Peer/Cultural interview -> Executive review.
2. **Standardized Scoring:** Using a rubric or scorecard to evaluate candidates against the same competencies.
3. **Availability Management:** Integrating ATS calendars with Google/Outlook to manage the complex scheduling of multi-person panels.
4. **Candidate Feedback:** Capturing structured notes from interviewers immediately after the call to prevent "Recency Bias."

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Decision Analysis:** Synthesizing the scores from multiple interviewers to identify the "Overall Fit."
*   **Process Analysis:** Analyzing the "Interview-to-Offer" ratio—are we interviewing too many people to find one good hire?

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Interview Rubric Spec:** The data structure of the digital scorecard used in the ATS.
*   **Scheduling Service Interface:** For the automated calendar sync for external candidates.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Unconscious Bias". Without a structured scorecard, interviewers tend to hire people who are "Like them." As a BA, you must insist that every interview has a pre-defined set of questions and that the "Scorecard" is mandatory. This not only improves hire quality but also protects the company legally during audit/compliance reviews.
