# Procurement Planning

Directly aligns with "Plan for Procurement" (APQC 4.1.1) and "Develop Sourcing Strategy" (APQC 4.1.2). This is the strategic layer of the Supply Chain.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Demand Aggregation:** Consolidating requirements from across the enterprise to achieve volume discounts.
2. **Category Analysis:** segmenting spend by complexity and risk (Kraljic Matrix).
3. **Sourcing Strategy:** Deciding whether to utilize a Single, Dual, or Multi-source strategy for key commodities.
4. **Market Intelligence:** Analyzing global supply trends, inflationary risks, and geopolitical constraints.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Decision Analysis:** using the "Sourcing Decision Tree" to choose between internal vs external procurement.
*   **Stakeholder Collaboration:** aligning Treasury's "Cash Conservation" goals with Ops' "Stock Availability" needs.
*   **Market Analysis:** benchmarking supplier pricing against global index rates (e.g. LME for metals).

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Maverick Spend." As a BA, design your procurement portal so it is easier for employees to buy from authorized vendors than it is to buy from Amazon. High friction in the official tool drives people to shadow procurement.
