# Customer Onboarding

In the FinTech domain, **Customer Onboarding** is the critical first interaction between the financial institution and a new client. It involves collecting demographic data, verifying identity (KYC/KYB), and performing initial risk profiling to ensure regulatory compliance before granting access to financial products.

## Node Knowledge: The Onboarding Lifecycle
1. **Application & Data Collection:** Gathering personal or business details securely via digital channels.
2. **Identity Verification (KYC/KYB):** Validating government IDs, biometrics, and corporate registries.
3. **Risk Scoring:** Assigning a risk profile based on geography, income source, and intended product usage.
4. **Account Provisioning:** Approving the customer and creating the initial ledger records.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

According to the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering onboarding heavily relies on **Requirements Elicitation** and **Requirements Life Cycle Management**.

*   **Process Modeling:** Mapping the happy path of instant approval vs. the exception paths (e.g., manual compliance review required).
*   **Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs):** Defining strict NFRs for security, data privacy (GDPR/CCPA/PIPL), and system latency during automated identity checks.
*   **Third-Party API Integration Analysis:** Determining the data contracts between the core system and third-party KYC providers.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Data Mapping Document:** Tracking PII (Personally Identifiable Information) from the UI down to the encrypted database.
*   **API Interface Specifications:** Defining the payload structure for identity verification calls.
*   **Risk Matrix Rules:** Documenting the business rules that trigger different risk tiers.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't underestimate the friction caused by compliance. Your goal is to maximize conversion rates while satisfying legal requirements. Always ask: "Can this data point be gathered passively in the background, or do we really need to put it on a form and interrupt the user?" 
> *A 5% drop-off at the ID scan step equals millions in lost revenue.*
