# Core Ledger & Accounts

The **Core Ledger & Accounts** system is the beating heart of any FinTech platform. It acts as the immutable system of record for all customer balances, transaction histories, and financial state. A robust core ledger ensures that money is never created or destroyed erroneously, adhering to strict double-entry accounting principles.

## Node Knowledge: Core Ledger Dynamics
1. **Account Structure:** Defining account hierarchies (e.g., checking, savings, sweeping accounts).
2. **Double-Entry Bookkeeping:** Ensuring every debit has a corresponding credit across the platform's internal and customer accounts.
3. **Balance Calculations:** Managing available balance vs. current balance (accounting for pending holds or authorizations).
4. **Interest & Fee Calculations:** Applying daily accruals and monthly capitalizing of interest or account fees.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In the *BABOK®* framework, the **Strategy Analysis** and **Requirements Analysis and Design Definition** knowledge areas are paramount.

*   **Data Modeling:** Designing the entity-relationship diagrams for accounts, transactions, and ledgers.
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** Capturing the logic for how and when funds are placed on hold, and the sequence of posting transactions (e.g., debits before credits).
*   **State Machine Design:** Mapping the lifecycle of a transaction from `Pending` to `Settled` or `Reversed`.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **State Transition Diagrams:** Visualizing the statuses an account or transaction can hold.
*   **Accounting Schema Document:** Detailing the Chart of Accounts (CoA) and mapping product events to specific ledger postings.
*   **Edge-case Scenarios Matrix:** Documenting what happens handling race conditions (e.g., two simultaneous withdrawals on a low balance).

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> The biggest mistake BAs make when designing core banking features is ignoring "Pending" states. Money rarely moves instantly. 
> Always account for the concept of *Holds*. The fundamental equation you must drill into your development team is:
> `Available Balance = Current Balance - Holds/Pending Transactions`.
