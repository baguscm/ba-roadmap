# Asset Maintenance Linkage

In **Advanced P2P with Assets**, **Maintenance Linkage** is the bridge between the Financial Asset (fixed asset) and the Operational Asset (equipment/machinery). This ensures that every dollar spent on maintenance or upgrades is correctly linked to the asset's total life-cycle cost and capitalization status.

## Node Knowledge: The Operational Connection
1. **Equipment Master:** Mapping the Financial Fixed Asset to a specific, unique piece of Equipment in the maintenance module.
2. **Maintenance Orders:** Tracking spare parts, labor, and services used to keep the asset operational.
3. **Subsequent Capitalization:** Identifying when a major repair actually increases the asset's value and should be added to the balance sheet.
4. **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):** Aggregating purchase cost, depreciation, and all historical maintenance costs.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't over-capitalize. Many maintenance teams try to capitalize every major repair to protect their budgets. As a BA, you must ensure that only "Value-Enhancing" repairs hit the balance sheet. If a repair just restores the asset to its *original* condition, it's an expense. If it *extends* the life or *increases* the capacity, it's capital.
