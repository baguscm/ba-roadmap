# Smart Grid Operations

Aligned with "Manage Logistics" (APQC 6.0) and "Maintain Grid Infrastructure" (APQC 6.4) in the Utility Management framework. This is the nervous system of the power network.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Network Monitoring:** Utilizing SCADA and IoT sensors to maintain real-time visibility into grid frequency and voltage.
2. **Outage Management (OMS):** Automating the detection and reporting of power failures (Outages) to restoration crews.
3. **Automated Restoration (FLISR):** Executing "Fault Location, Isolation, and Service Restoration" (FLISR) to re-route power around a fault within seconds.
4. **AMI Data Capture:** Managing the "Advanced Metering Infrastructure" (Smart Meters) that bi-directionally communicate with the customer edge.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Interface Analysis:** mapping the data flow between field sensors (IEDs), the central SCADA system, and the customer billing portal.
*   **Prototyping:** designing intuitive "Grid Visualization" UIs that allow operators to see hotspots and load-imbalances instantly.
*   **Risk Analysis:** modeling the vulnerability of the grid to cyber-attacks or severe weather events (e.g. wildfires).

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Focus on "Interoperability." As a BA, ensure your field devices follow standard protocols like DNP3 or IEC 61850. If you build a proprietary, non-standard grid, you will be locked into a single vendor for 30 years.
