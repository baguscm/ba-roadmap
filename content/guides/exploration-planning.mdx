# Exploration & Planning

The fundamental starting point of the Mining lifecycle, mapped to "Manage External Relationships & Environment" (APQC 11.0) and "Develop Product Strategy" (APQC 1.0) for the extractive industry.

## Node Knowledge: The Process Lifecycle
1. **Geological Surveying:** Using remote sensing and physical sampling to identify potential ore deposits.
2. **Resource Modeling:** Creating a "Digital Twin" of the underground deposit (Block Model) to estimate grade and volume.
3. **Mine Design:** Planning the physical infrastructure (Open-pit vs Underground) and the "Life of Mine" (LOM) schedule.
4. **Feasibility & ESG:** Conducting complex Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) assessments to obtain a "Social License to Operate."

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

In alignment with the *Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK®)*, mastering this node relies on the following knowledge areas and techniques:

*   **Risk Analysis:** modeling the impact of commodity price volatility on the "Break-even" point of the mine.
*   **Survey/Map Analysis:** interpreting geospatial and topographical data to define optimal site layouts.
*   **Financial Analysis:** calculating the "Net Present Value" (NPV) of the multi-decade mining project.

## 💡 Expert BA Review & Adjustments

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Respect the "Closure" from Day One. As a BA, don't just plan for extraction. You must design the data models for "Reclamation" at the same time. If you don't have a plan to return the land to its original state, you will never get the regulatory approval to start digging.
