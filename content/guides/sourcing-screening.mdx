# Sourcing & Screening

**Sourcing & Screening** is the top-of-funnel talent acquisition process. It involves proactively searching for candidates, advertising the role across multiple job boards, and using standardized filters to identify the most qualified individuals for the next stage.

## Node Knowledge: The Talent Funnel
1. **Multi-channel Sourcing:** Posting to LinkedIn, Indeed, niche job boards, and employee referral programs.
2. **Resume Parsing / Parsing (AI):** Using an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) to automatically extract skills and experience from resumes.
3. **Knockout Questions:** Automated screening questions (e.g., "Do you have a valid work visa?") to disqualify ineligible candidates immediately.
4. **Initial HR Screening:** Preliminary phone or video calls to assess cultural fit and salary expectations.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Solution Evaluation:** Analyzing the "Quality of Hire" from different sourcing channels—do candidates from LinkedIn perform better than those from Indeed?
*   **Process Analysis:** Identifying bottlenecks in the "Time to Screen" that cause top talent to be snapped up by competitors.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Sourcing Channel Performance Report:** A dashboard showing conversion rates for every job board used.
*   **Screening Workflow Diagram:** Detailing the automated vs. manual touchpoints for a new applicant.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't over-rely on AI screening. Many "Great" candidates are rejected by ATS systems because their resumes aren't formatted for robots. As a BA, your goal is to ensure the "Parsing Logic" isn't too rigid. You should advocate for a "Human-in-the-Loop" fallback for high-seniority or specialized technical roles to ensure you aren't missing hidden gems.
