# Production Scheduling & Capacity

While MRP identifies *what* to make, **Production Scheduling** determines *when* and *on which machine* it should be made. It is the process of sequencing manufacturing orders to optimize machine utilization and labor while meeting delivery deadlines.

## Node Knowledge: The Timing Optimization
1. **Work Centers:** Defining the physical machines, production lines, or teams.
2. **Capacities:** Calculating how many "Standard Hours" a work center can produce per day.
3. **Sequencing:** Determining the order of jobs to minimize "Changeover Time" (e.g., grouping products of the same color or size).
4. **Finite vs. Infinite Scheduling:** Scheduling based on realistic resource limits vs. assuming unlimited capacity.
5. **Leveling:** Smoothing out production "peaks" and "valleys" to ensure a consistent workload.

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Requirements Modeling:** Defining the "Standard Times" for each operation—how long does it *really* take to make one unit?
*   **Business Rules Analysis:** Defining the priority logic—for example, does a "Rush Order" automatically bump a standard order in the schedule?

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Gantt Chart View Spec:** Designing how planners visualize the schedule and move jobs between machines.
*   **Setup Time Matrix:** Detailing the time required to switch from Product A to Product B.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Don't over-schedule. A machine scheduled at 100% capacity is a disaster waiting to happen. The slightest delay (a broken tool, a sick employee) will cause a domino effect through your entire supply chain. As a BA, recommend a "Buffer Capacity" of 10-15% to allow for the inevitable variability of the shop floor.
