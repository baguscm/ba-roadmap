# Sales Order Entry

**Order Entry** is the point of conversion where a customer's request becomes a legally binding commitment to deliver goods or services. In the **APQC** framework, this is part of the "Manage Customer Orders" process group.

## Node Knowledge: The Fulfillment Trigger
1. **Quotation to Order:** Converting a negotiated price into a confirmed demand.
2. **Availability Check (ATP):** Verifying "Available to Promise" inventory before committing a delivery date.
3. **Price Determination:** Applying discounts, taxes, and shipping surcharges.
4. **Order Header & Line Items:** Structure of the order data (Customer info vs. Product info).

## Key BA Activities (BABOK® Alignment)

*   **Financial Analysis:** Ensuring the order margins meet company profitability targets before approval.
*   **Requirements Validation:** Ensuring "Special Instructions" (e.g., liftgate delivery) are captured as actionable data for the warehouse.

## Typical Deliverables

*   **Process Flow Diagram:** Showing the "Happy Path" from order receipt to warehouse release.
*   **Business Rules Document:** Detailing how the system handles price conflicts or minimum order quantities (MOQ).

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> The biggest O2C bottleneck is "Order Holds". If an order is held for credit or missing data, nobody gets paid. As a BA, your job is to automate as much validation as possible at the point of entry so that orders flow straight through to fulfillment without human intervention.
