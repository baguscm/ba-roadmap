# E-Com Front-to-Back Sync

In **Advanced B2C**, **Front-to-Back Sync** is the critical glue between the digital storefront and the physical supply chain. It ensures that data flows seamlessly between the customer's web experience and the backend ERP system for inventory, pricing, and order fulfillment.

## Node Knowledge: The Omni-channel Sync
1. **Inventory Visibility:** Pushing real-time stock levels from the warehouse to the web storefront to prevent overselling.
2. **Order Injection:** Mapping the website checkout payload into the ERP Sales Order structure.
3. **Status Feedback:** Pushing fulfillment updates (Shipped, Tracking #, Out for Delivery) back to the customer's online account.
4. **Master Data Sync:** Ensuring product names, descriptions, and prices are identical on both platforms.

## 💡 Expert BA Review

> [!TIP]
> **From the Desk of a Lead Business Analyst**
> Beware of "Race Conditions". If two customers buy the last item at the exact same millisecond, which order fails? As a BA, you must define the "Inventory Lock" logic—does a cart hold an item? Or is it first-come, first-served at the "Place Order" click?
